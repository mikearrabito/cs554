Pontiac Aztek
A mid-size crossover SUV made by General Motors from 2001 to 2005. This vehicle has a V-6 engine and 4-speed automatic transmission. Although the concept car was well accepted, sales for the production version were well under expectations. GM forecast sales of up to 75,000 Azteks per year and needed to produce 30,000 annually to break even. Just 27,793 were sold in 2002, which was the model's best-selling year.
L'Œuf électrique
Also known as "The Electric Egg" in English.
This vehicle was made in 1942 and was very ahead of its time. It is very lightweight, making use of materials such as aluminum for the body and plexiglass for a total weight of 350 kg. This vehicle was originally made with an electric motor only, and later was given a single-cylinder gas engine.
General Motors Firebird 1 XP-21
Prototype car developed by General Motors in 1953 which used a jet engine. The car's engine was a 370 HP Whirlfire Turbo Power gas turbine engine. The car weighed 2,500 lb. The vehicle was a single seater, with wings on the sides and a tail fin, emulating a jet. GM never tried to test the vehicle's top speed, instead they were just testing if the turbine engine was feasible for land vehicles.
Tesla Cybertruck
Upcoming pickup truck by Tesla that is powered fully electrically. The truck will have a towing capacity of 7,500 lbs, ground clearance of 16", and 100 cu ft of storage space, making this a very useful utility vehicle. The range for the base model is estimated at 250+ miles.
Helicron
This is an open-wheel vehicle that uses a propeller to drive. This is a one-of-one vehicle that originally used a two cylinder engine. This vehicle only weighs 1000 lbs. This vehicle is steered and driven by the rear wheels. The top speed is 75 mph.
Stout Scarab
This is a 1930-1940 V8 powered rear-engine vehicle created by Stout Motor Car Company considered to be the first minivan. This was also the world's first car with a fiberglass body and air suspension.
Citroën Karin
Citroën created this concept car for its 1980 Paris Salon stand to attract crowds to view it. This car was a 3-seater with the driver's seat located in the middle of the two passengers. The car had butterfly doors and a striking pyramidal design.
Suzuki X-90
This ultra compact car by Suzuki was only produced from 1995-1997. It featured a 4 cylinder engine making 95 hp. There was a five speed manual or 4 speed automatic transmission as options. Only 7,205 X-90s were imported to the United States.
BMW Isetta
An Italian designed microcar that was the world's first production car to reach 3L/100km fuel consumption (78 mpg). It was the top selling single cylinder car in the world with 161,728 cars sold. The car was initially created by Italian company Iso, and later built under license by others.
Cadillac Cyclone
A concept car created by Cadillac which featured a 390 cu in V8 engine, rear-mounted transaxle, and all-wheel independent suspension. A unique feature of this car was the car's exhaust pipes were just ahead of the front wheels. The two pointed cones at the front of the car were not just design features, they were actually radar sensors that were part of a collision avoidance system. Such a system was still in its very early stages of development.
